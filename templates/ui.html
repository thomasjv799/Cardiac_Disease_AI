About Cardiac arrest

An estimated 17.9 million people died from Cardio Vascular Diseases in 2019, representing 32% of all global deaths. In cardiac arrest for example, the heart abruptly stops beating. Without prompt intervention, it can result in the person's death. The main symptom is loss of consciousness and unresponsiveness. Most cardiovascular diseases can be prevented by addressing behavioural risk factors such as tobacco use, unhealthy diet and obesity, physical inactivity and harmful use of alcohol.
